Welcome to Kasauli

Nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Shivalik range, Kasauli is a charming hill station in the Solan district of Himachal Pradesh.

Known for its serene beauty, colonial architecture, and panoramic views of the Himalayas, this picturesque town is a popular destination for tourists seeking peace and tranquility. Kasauli was established by the British in the mid-19th century and served as a summer retreat for colonial officials. Today, it is a quaint hill station that retains much of its colonial-era charm, with narrow winding roads, old churches, and sprawling bungalows. Whether you're looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life, take a leisurely stroll through the town's quaint bazaars, or simply bask in the beauty of the Himalayas, Kasauli has something to offer everyone.

Tourist attractions in Kasauli

    Monkey Point

    This is the highest point in Kasauli and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. The place is named after the monkey god Hanuman, and there is a small temple dedicated to him at the top.

    Gurkha Fort

    This is a historic fort located on a hilltop in Kasauli. It was built in the 19th century by the Gurkha army and is now in ruins. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the valley from the fort.

    Gilbert Trail

    This is a scenic walking trail that takes visitors through the lush forests of Kasauli. It is a great place to spot birds and wildlife.

    Kasauli Brewery

    This is the oldest distillery and brewery in Asia, established in 1820. Visitors can take a guided tour of the brewery and sample some of the finest liquors.
